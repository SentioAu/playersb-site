Core principles

  • Normalize before comparing: raw totals can mislead when minutes differ, so rate-based metrics are used where appropriate.
  • Explainable outputs: when a score or indicator is shown, its inputs are visible and understandable.
  • No predictions: PlayersB does not publish tips, “locks”, or guaranteed outcomes.

Key concepts

Per-90 metrics

Per-90 answers a simple question: “What would this player’s output look like over a full match?” It allows fair comparison between players who have played different numbers of minutes.

Efficiency vs volume

Some players produce through volume (more shots, more touches), while others produce through efficiency (higher conversion on fewer actions). A meaningful comparison shows both dimensions.

Consistency

Consistency describes how stable a player’s output is across matches. A simple approach measures variation around an average: lower variation indicates more stable performance.

How this connects to PlayersB tools

  • Player pages apply these concepts to individual profiles.
  • Comparison tools use normalized metrics to highlight tradeoffs between players.
  • Fantasy-safe tools apply scoring rules transparently without recommendations.
